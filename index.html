About Us!

Camel!

  1. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  3. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

Pigeon!

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  2. Pigeons are highly sociable animals.
  3. Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.

Panda!

  1. A giant panda is much bigger than your teddy bear.
  2. Pandas go from pink to white and black
  3. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

Cicada!

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs
  2. Most have red-orange eyes.
  3. Their short adult life is not unusual for bugs

Teddy!

  1. The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  2. This plush is a symbol of love, nurture, innocence, and childhood.
  3. The teddy bear is a very traditional and timeless toy, and is often given as a keepsake to a young child

Chameleon!

  1. Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads.
  2. Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues
  3. Most chameleons eat on things you wouldn't want to find on your dinner plate.
